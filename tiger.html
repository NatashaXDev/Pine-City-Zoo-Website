TIGER

The tiger (Panthera tigris) is the largest member of the cat family and one of the most iconic animals in the world. With its bold black stripes over fiery orange fur, the tiger is a symbol of strength, agility, and raw power. Native to parts of Asia, tigers inhabit a variety of environments, from dense forests and mangrove swamps to grasslands and even snowy mountains.

Tigers are solitary animals and expert hunters, relying on stealth, strength, and powerful senses to catch their prey. A single adult tiger can consume up to 40 kg of meat in one night, often feeding on deer, wild boar, and other large mammals. Despite their fearsome reputation, tigers are incredibly graceful and are known to swim long distances, a unique trait among big cats.

Sadly, all tiger subspecies are either endangered or critically endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and conflict with humans. Zoos and wildlife conservation programs play a vital role in protecting these magnificent animals through breeding programs, habitat preservation efforts, and public education.
